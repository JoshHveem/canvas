Nib Sanding
Dirt nibs in a refinished panel can usually be removed during the detailing process. Some of the tools to remove them include a small sanding block and wet sandpaper, a self-contained abrasive block, and an electric denibbing tool. When using any of these tools, always start with the least aggressive grit that will do the job. First, lets start with this small sanding block. The sanding is contained to this single nib. The panel and sandpaper are wet with a spray bottle. We’ll use light pressure and sand in all directions. Progress is checked by removing the water with a squeegee. Sanding is continued until the dirt nib is level with the surrounding area. Next we’ll use these abrasive blocks. When using these blocks, it’s recommended they be wet, and even soaked in water. The sanding is again contained to this single nib. Water is sprayed on the panel. Light pressure is also used with the block, sanding again in all directions. Progress is checked by removing the water with a squeegee. As we can see, we need to do more sanding, indicated by this halo. The sanding is continued until the dirt nib is level with the surrounding area. On this nib, let’s use the electric denibbing tool. When using this tool, use light down pressure and try not to collapse the foam backing on the sanding disc. While sanding, the tool is moved in a small circular motion around the nib. Progress is checked, and sanding is continued until the nib is level with the surrounding area. When denibbing a refinish, it’s important to keep the repair area as small as possible and work on one single nib at a time. The sanded area would then be followed up with buffing and polishing.
