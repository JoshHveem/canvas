Wet Sanding
One technique that is commonly used in detailing to remove refinish defects is hand wet sanding. We’ll be using a film thickness gauge, wet sandpaper, sanding pads, a squeegee, a water spray bottle, and a bucket of water to demonstrate this. We’ll start by taking initial film thickness measurements on the entire area to be sanded to ensure there’s enough clearcoat to work with. Start with the least aggressive sanding grit that will do the job. In this case, P1500. The sandpaper is cut to the proper size for the pad. Some product makers may require pre-soaking the sandpaper. Always use a pad when wet sanding to keep the sandpaper flat. Wet the surface with water and sand perpendicular to the bodylines. If you feel or hear dirt underneath the sandpaper immediately stop and squeegee off the panel. One spec of dirt can severely scratch the surface. We’ll check our progress by using a squeegee to remove the water and sanding sludge. Once the defect is removed, we’ll switch to a finer grit sandpaper. This is to remove the P1500 sandscratches. This time we’ll be sanding parallel to the bodyline. Sanding is this direction will allow us to easily see when the sandscratches are removed. Next the panel is squeegeed off. Note that there only sandscratches parallel to the bodyline. This area is now ready for buffing and polishing.
