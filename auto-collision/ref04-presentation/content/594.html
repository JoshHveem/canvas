Removing A Stain
Today we’ll look at some common stain removal recommendations on this water-based coffee stain. A basic stain removal kit includes a clean absorbent cloth or towel, and a stain removal solution in a spray bottle, such as all-purpose cleaner and water for water-based stains. We’ll apply the cleaning solution to the stain. The stain entered the carpet as a liquid, and we’re simply turning it back into a liquid. Bunch up the clean cloth and proceed to blot the stained area. Don’t rub the stain, this may spread it out and push it further into the carpet backing. You’ll start to see the stain transferring into the cloth. If the stain doesn’t come all the way out, a second application of cleaner may be needed. Cleaner is re-applied and the stain is re-blotted. Next the area is sprayed with water and blotted up with a clean portion of the cloth. The cleaned area is now allowed to dry. If this were a petroleum-based stain, a petroleum-based solvent cleaner would be used instead of the all-purpose water-based cleaner. Cleaning may need to be followed up with an odor neutralizer.
