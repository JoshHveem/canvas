Replacing Hinged Liftgate Glass
Many sport utility vehicles have hinged movable glass located at the rear hatch, or liftgate. Letâ€™s see how this glass is replaced.

First, a visual inspection is made and a repair plan is developed. The wiring plug is disconnected from the defroster grid by pressing the release tab and pulling the plug from the spade. The lifting struts are disconnected by prying their retaining clips outward and unsnapping the struts from the ball studs on the vehicle. With the help of an assistant, the fasteners holding the hinges to the vehicle are removed and the glass is lifted from the opening.

The glass is placed on a padded bench and all parts requiring transfer to the replacement glass are removed. Notice the plastic protective washers located between the glass and the parts being removed. The parts are then installed on the replacement glass, taking care to ensure that the plastic washers are properly positioned. Using a torque wrench, all fasteners are torqued to the proper specifications. Before the glass is installed, the weatherstripping is inspected for damage.

With the help of an assistant, the glass is positioned in the opening and the fasteners that hold the hinge to the vehicle are installed. Then the lifting struts are snapped over the ball studs on the vehicle and the wiring plug is pushed over the spade on the defroster grid until the release tab locks in place. The glass is closed and inspected for proper fit and operation to complete the replacement procedure.
