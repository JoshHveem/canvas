To avoid the possibility of the glass falling out and breaking when the fasteners are removed, it is first supported from the outside by securely taping it to the vehicle at the top. The quarter panel upper interior trim is then carefully removed to gain access to the fasteners for the glass panel. Removing the front panel reveals that ductwork for the rear heat and air conditioning unit is blocking access to the front fasteners. In order to remove the ductwork, the quarter panel lower interior trim is removed.

With the trim panel removed, the rear heat and air conditioning unit is visible in the quarter panel inner structure. The latch is separated by carefully prying the ball on the vehicle side out of the socket on the glass side. The nuts holding the front glass hinges to the vehicle are then removed. The tape supporting the glass is removed and the glass is carefully lifted from the vehicle.

With the glass removed, the weatherstripping should now be inspected for damage. If a replacement glass is being installed, any necessary parts such as the hinges or latch should be transferred as a bench operation. The glass is now carefully repositioned in the vehicle and supported along the top by taping it securely to the vehicle. The nuts holding the hinges to the vehicle are installed and torqued to their proper specification. The two halves of the latch are reconnected by securely snapping the ball back into the socket.

After verifying proper glass panel alignment and operation, and testing for water leaks, the rear heat and air conditioning ductwork and quarter panel interior trim are reinstalled to complete the repair.
