Breaking Glass
This windshield is a typical piece of laminated safety glass. Let’s attempt to break it by striking it with a pointed punch. Notice the toughness of the glass as the first hit fails to break it. The second strike breaks the glass into large pieces that are held intact by the inner plastic laminate layer. This type of damage is typical of laminated safety glass.

Next, let’s see how a piece of tempered safety glass breaks. This minivan quarter panel vent glass is placed in a plastic bag for safety reasons before the glass is broken. It’s struck with the same punch that was used to break the laminated windshield glass. Notice how the glass explodes into small fragments. While the tempering process toughens the glass it also places it in tension. Once the hardened outer skin of the glass is penetrated, the glass shatters. This is the typical type of damage that results.
