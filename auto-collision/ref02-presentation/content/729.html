Preparing A Part For Primer
The repair on this door is ready to be prepared for the application of primer-surfacer. To begin, the area around the repair will be sanded with P240 grit sandpaper on a dual action or DA sander. This will help remove the deeper P180 grit sandscratches, as well as extend our featheredge around this area. Be sure to check the speed of the sander before placing it on the panel. The sander is placed on the panel and then turned on, keeping the sander as flat as possible. Progress should be checked often, removing as little material as possible. Next, sandpaper is switched to P320 grit. Sanding is done in the same fashion, this time removing the previous sandscratches. The area is blown off with clean, dry compressed air and wiped clean. Now that we have a gradual featheredge, finer sandscratches and the repair area is clean, we can now begin masking for primer.
