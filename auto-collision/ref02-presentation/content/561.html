Lift Tape Masking
This vehicle is ready to be masked for refinishing. When masking this vehicle, we’ll show using several different techniques and materials. To start, all stationary glass mating flanges are masked off as no topcoats should be applied to these areas. Now we’re going to mask the quarter glass. When masking the quarter glass, we’re going to use trim masking or lift tape. The lift tape contains a hard plastic edge with an adhesive on the other side. A piece is measured and cut from the roll. The piece is then slid between the glass molding and the vehicle. Once in, the tape backing is removed, the adhesive portion is pulled down and adhered to the glass. This lifts and holds the trim away from the vehicle so refinish material doesn’t leave a hard tape line next to the glass. A strip is cut for the corner portion of the glass. Relief cuts are made in a small strip of the lift tape to help mask the corner of the glass. Then additional masking is applied to the quarter glass edge. Finally, the lift tape is secured to the window glass using masking tape.
