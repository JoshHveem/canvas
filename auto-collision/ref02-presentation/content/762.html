Preparing Bare Plastic Parts
Some plastic parts, such as this bumper cover, come unprimed. Unprimed plastics requires special preparation techniques to help clean off the mold release agents as well as to help with the adhesion of refinish materials. We’re going to be using this kit, designed for preparing unprimed plastic parts. The kit includes a presaturated sponge and scuff pad. We’ll scrub the entire part to clean it, as well as degloss the part. The part is then rinsed with water and dried using a lint-free towel. Next, a nonstatic cleaner and plastic adhesion promoter wipe is used. This is applied over the entire part. We’ll wipe in one direction, minimizing overlap. After allowing adequate flash time, a plastic adhesion promoter is sprayed in a light coat over the entire bumper cover. Once the adhesion promoter has flashed, the bumper is now ready for the application of sealer as well as topcoats.
