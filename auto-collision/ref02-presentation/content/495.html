Reverse Masking
Around the tail light pocket, tape is applied to the panel and rolled back to create a soft edge. The same technique is used to mask the bottom of the quarter panel. Here we see the soft edge created by the rolled tape. This technique is called reverse masking. Letâ€™s take a look at using it to mask off the doors on this side of the vehicle. To start, the gasket on top of the doors is taped down, which makes more room to allow for the reverse masking. Tape is applied to the roof pillar along the door openings. It is also applied to the dogleg area and the rocker panel. The doors are then closed, making sure the tape edge ends up on the outside of the doors. The tape is then rolled back and taped down to the doors. This helps create a soft edge, eliminating hard tapelines.
