Proper Spray Gun Technique
Proper spray gun technique is in part a combination of overlap of the pattern, distance from the panel, and travel speed. The variations in these depend on the product and manufacturer being sprayed and the effect you’re trying to create. Let’s see how variations of each affect the refinish. We’re going to do a demonstration on spray gun techniques using this white box side and blue basecoat. We’re using the blue basecoat so that we can see the different variations using the different techniques. Generally, a 75% overlap is called for, which means that each successive pass is overlapped by 75%. Depending on the product you’re spraying, it may be recommended to do a tighter overlap of 85-90% with each pass. Now let’s take a look at the effects of the variations of a spray gun distance to the panel. Using the same travel speed and overlap, we’ll exaggerate our gun distance and have it too far from the panel. Here we can see the spotty coverage and poor hiding. It also causes excessive overspray. Here we can see what happens when the spray gun is way too close to the surface. The area just refinished reveals that the coverage is too wet and heavy. This could produce runs and sags in the finish. Here we’re spraying at the recommended distance of six to eight inches from the panel. We can see we achieve proper coverage. Next we’ll use the correct gun distance and overlap, but vary our travel speed and see how this affects the finish. Here we’re using a travel speed that is too fast. Notice the results are similar to the distance being too far from the panel. Not enough material is deposited on the surface. Now we’re using a travel speed that is too slow. Notice how heavy the material is and it begins to sag. Here we can see how the proper travel speed gives us even coverage. When spraying refinish materials, proper trigger technique should be used. Make sure to trigger on as you begin the stroke, and off at the end of the stroke. This helps reduce the amount of materials deposited at the edge of the panels. It’s difficult showing each of these factors separately as they all work together when refinishing a vehicle. It’s important to develop your own style and technique depending on the product you’re using and what you’re trying to accomplish.
