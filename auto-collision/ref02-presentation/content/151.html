Preparing Bare And Repaired Steel
This area has been prepped, masked, and cleaned and is ready for the application of primer-surfacer. To begin, we first need to restore corrosion protection to the exposed bare metal. We’re going to apply self-etching primer using this etch pen and allow it to dry. Next, we’ll apply the first coat of primer-surfacer to the widest point of the repair area using the reverse priming technique. Doing this will reduce the overspray edges and help us create an easier feather into the existing finish. The primer is applied using the recommended fluid tip and gun pressure. Overlap each pass about 50%, staying about eight inches away from the panel. After allowing the coat to flash for the recommended time, a second coat is applied slightly inward from the first coat. Again after flash, a third and final coat is applied inward of the last coat. After the primer is allowed to cure, guide coat is applied. We’ll begin sanding the primer-surfacer using P320 grit sandpaper on a sanding block. Sanding is done at a slight angle across the panel. Crosscutting your pattern can ensure the panel is sanded evenly. The sanding is extended just beyond the repair area to start feathering the edge. Once all the primer-surfacer is flat and all edges are feathered, we’ll switch to a finer P400 grit sandpaper. We’ll be using a smaller and softer block with the 400 as we’re just removing the P320 grit sandscratches and helping to feather the edge. After sanding is complete, masking can be removed and the area can be wiped clean. This panel is now ready to be prepared for refinishing.
