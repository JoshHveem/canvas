Foam Tape Masking
Foam masking tape can be used to mask and seal off jambs as well as other parts of the vehicle. We’ll be using it to mask and seal off the doorjambs on this side of the vehicle. The adhesive is on one side of the foam. The adhesive portion should always be placed towards the inside of the panel. If we’re painting the door, as well as the quarter panel, we would need to apply foam around the entire edge, and the masking would be done. However, since we’re not painting the door, tape will be applied to the edge of the door. We can use the foam tape to mask the area above the doors along the roof pillar. The tape is applied in one piece, being sure not to stretch it as stretching it will cause poor adhesion. Another single piece is applied along the bottoms of both doors. Using one continuous piece will help prevent overspray from entering. The rear door is closed. The foam tape is then tucked in to the desired depth with a spreader. This will help prevent any hard tape lines. The front door will be closed and the edges between the doors will be sealed in the booth. This will allow us to get in and out of the vehicle for driving it into the booth. When removing the foam masking, don’t peel it off like conventional masking. This could leave adhesive behind. Instead, grab one end and pull.
