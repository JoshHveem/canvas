Weld-On Dent Removal

One common way of removing damage from panels is using a weld-on dent removal tool. This is an attachment for a spot welder, which welds on studs. This is a portable stud gun, or stud welder, which also welds on studs. The stud is then inserted into the slidehammer to remove the damage. This pry tool can also be used.

When using weld-on dent removal tools, it’s important to verify that there's no flammable material on the backside of the area to be welded. The vehicle is covered to protect the interior as well as adjacent panels. Because weld-on dent removal tools need a ground to complete the weld, refinish coatings are removed from the damaged area. First, the stud is placed into the end of a stud gun. The gun is brought up to the panel and the head of the stud is set against the damage. The stud gun is then pushed forward to ensure contact between the bare metal and the ground collar. The trigger is then squeezed briefly to weld the pin in place. Studs will be placed starting on the outside of the dent working your way inward to the deepest point of the dent using the “last in, first out” rule. Once a stud is welded in place, a slidehammer is attached to it. The slidehammer is used to raise the low spots using consecutive light blows. Heavy blows shouldn’t be used as they can cause damage and the steel has memory and wants to return to shape. After initial slidehammering, a body hammer is used to strike the high spots while outward pressure is applied to the slidehammer. We’ll continue to raise the low spots using the studs and slidehammer. A pry tool is also used to help aid in raising the lows. Use light pressure, taking caution not to over-pry. Whenever using weld-on dent removal tools, it’s important to corrosion-protect the inside of the panel, as coatings will be burned off. This area is now ready to be prepared for body filler.
