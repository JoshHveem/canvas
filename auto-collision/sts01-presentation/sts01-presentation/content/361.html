Hammer Maintenance

If hammers are not properly maintained, surface imperfections on the face of the hammer can transfer to the panel being straightened and hinder the straightening process. We’ll start by using this file to remove surface imperfections on the face of the hammer, making sure to file in all directions. Filing in only one direction will result in an uneven face. This is what the face of the hammer should look like after filing.

Now we’ll use 600-grit sandpaper to polish the face of the hammer. An oilstone could also be used. Again, sanding should be done in all directions. After polishing, the hammer should look similar to this. The hammer can now be checked to see if maintenance is complete. Marking paint is applied to the face of the hammer. Then the hammer is struck against a flat piece of steel. Observe the paint transfer. The paint should come off of the center of the hammer face. If not, more maintenance is required. Once the paint transfers from the center of the hammer face, the hammer maintenance is complete, and the hammer can be again used for straightening.
