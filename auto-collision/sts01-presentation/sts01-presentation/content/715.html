Repairing Damage To A Complex Area

Damage in an area with multiple contours such as this can be repaired using a variety of techniques. We’ll begin the straightening process by removing the damage to this body feature line. This is done because the feature line gives the part shape and strength. Damage to feature lines and body panel edges can distort adjacent areas. If straightening was started first in the adjacent area, it would become deformed again when we straighten the feature line. A stud gun has been chosen to remove the damage. After making sure that there aren't any flammable materials behind the repair area, the paint coatings are removed. Next, a stud is welded to the low spot on the feature line and a slidehammer is used to raise the low spot. This is followed by tapping down the high spots around the damage while applying outward pressure on the slidehammer. We’ll continue raising the low spots on the feature line. The slidehammer is used with consecutive light blows to raise the low area. Heavy blows can inflict damage to the area and do not need to be used as the steel has memory and wants to return to shape. The high spots are continued to be tapped down and also helps stress-relieve the body feature line. The area above the feature line is then straightened by starting on the outside of the dent and working towards the middle. The studs are all removed and ground flat so that progress can be checked.

Now that progress has been checked, and the feature line has been restored, we can continue the straightening process on the area adjacent, below the feature line. Again, we start straightening on the outside of the damage and work towards the middle. The damaged area is now ready to be prepared for body filler application. A DA sander with P-80 grit sandpaper is used to prep the repair area for application of filler. To assist in restoring the body feature line, tape is used to make the line more prominent. Filler is applied to the lower repair area. The tape is removed and the filler is allowed to cure. Tape is then reapplied to the body feature line and filler is applied to the area above the tape.

Sanding is done following the contour of the panel, occasionally crosscutting the area to ensure that the panel is sanded evenly. Any high spots are tapped down. After block sanding the flatter areas of the panel, a cylindrical sanding block is used above the body feature line. After checking progress, a second coat of filler is applied above and below the body feature line. The repair area is again sanded. The repair area is now ready to be featheredged and prepped for the application of primer-surfacer.
