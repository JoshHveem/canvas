Heat Shrinking

Occasionally during the straightening process, metal can become stretched, creating the “oil can” effect. There are several ways of heat shrinking metal to get rid of this effect. This can be done using an oxyacetylene torch, an attachment on a spot welder, a stud gun with a special tip, or an induction heater, which we’ll be using. Whenever heat shrinking, make sure that there’s a fire extinguisher nearby and you know how to properly use it. Also, a bucket of water and a clean cloth will be required. The induction heater is placed over the raised area and the trigger is squeezed. The heating element is moved around the stretched area in a circular pattern. Use caution as the area may heat rather quickly. Remove the heat and tap down the heated high spot with a body hammer. Finish by using a wet sponge or cloth to quick cool the area. After cooling, when the area is pressed, notice how the “oil canning” effect is gone.
