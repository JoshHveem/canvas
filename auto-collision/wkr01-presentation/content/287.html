I-CAR is committed to compliance with the antitrust laws of the United States and each of the jurisdictions in which it does business. At the meeting that you are attending may also be representatives of your competitors. It is imperative that you keep this in mind and be certain to take precautions not to violate, or even appear to violate, the antitrust laws.

The antitrust laws generally prohibit contracts, agreements, combinations or conspiracies which unreasonably restrain free competition. A focus of the antitrust laws is to prohibit agreements or understandings between competitors or potential competitors which restrict their freedom to independently decide which markets they will enter, what prices and terms they will offer, or which firms they will deal with. It is important to note that merely exchanging or disseminating sensitive information (such as pricing information) - even without an express agreement with a competitor - can violate the antitrust laws.

To that end, during this meeting and any interactions with competitors that occur during the course of this meeting (including opportunities to meet with competitors during I-CAR training classes), there will be no discussions or communications relating in any way to:

  • Prices or costs of any kind, including labor times to be applied or prices to be paid for materials and services;
  • General price trends, approaches to pricing, discounts, sales, rebates, bids, requests for quotations, or responses to customer requests;
  • Allocating, sharing, or dividing customers, markets or territories among competitors;
  • Refusing to deal with any competitor or third party; or
  • Limiting or restricting the volume, quantity, or type of goods or services made available to customers.

All such subjects are to be established unilaterally by each without discussion among or agreement with competitors. If any of these subjects arise or you are unsure whether a communication or discussion is prohibited, the conversation should be stopped.
