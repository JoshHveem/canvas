Safety With Corrosives

There is a certain paint surface condition where a corrosive material is sometimes recommended as part of the proper repair procedure. The condition is called rail dust, which occurs when tiny metal particles become imbedded in the paint surface. These metal particles cannot be buffed out. They must be chemically removed.

Some vehicle makers recommend using an oxalic acid-based wash. It’s important to read the label directions carefully. Acid-resistant gloves and eye goggles are required when using this oxalic acid material. The product SDS may specify other precautions, such as wearing an air-purifying respirator to prevent inhaling vapors during prolonged use.
