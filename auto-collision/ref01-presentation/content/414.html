Cleaning A Spray Gun
Today we will be showing how to manually clean a spray gun, using this waterborne gun wash system. This gun washer also has an automatic gun wash and an optional heat system to aid in the cleaning process. Depending on the paint maker, it may be recommended to use the heat system.To begin, turn on the air supply to the system. This washer has two types of manual gun cleaning tools. This tool, called a power pistol, provides a powerful stream of fluid to help flush the passages of the spray gun. And this tool, called a flow brush, dispenses cleaning fluid through these bristles to aid in scrubbing individual parts of the spray gun.First, we'll use the power pistol to flush out the fluid passages of the spray gun. Next, we'll remove the air cap and scrub it with the flow brush, making sure that all refinish materials are fully removed. The fluid nozzle is removed and cleaned in the same manner.Then the flow brush is used to clean the needle and fluid passages. After reassembling, the power pistol is used to flush the spray gun one last time.To complete cleaning, the spray gun should be thoroughly dried.
