Rebuilding A Spray Gun - Part 2
Before reassembling the spray gun, you want to make sure that all of our parts are clean and free of dirt and contamination. Now that the spray gun is cleaned, we can start installing the new parts from the rebuild kit and discard the old parts. The new air distribution ring is now installed. Next we are going to install the air piston retaining screw. Apply spray gun lubricant to the air piston. Reinstall the air micrometer assembly using the Allen wrench on the retaining screw. On the fan control assembly there is an O-ring that needs to be replaced. The new one was provided in the rebuild kit. Now that the O-ring has been replaced, we can install our fan control assembly. Replacing the fan control on a 3000 is fairly straightforward, however on the 4000 model there are a few differences. As you can see on this 4000 model, the fan control knob is aligned with the spray gun body. We are now going to remove the T-20 torx retaining screw. When replacing the fan control assembly on a 4000 model, make sure that the small brass spindle nut marker is aligned in the six o'clock position. Other than the fan control assembly on the 4000 model, the rebuild process is essentially the same for the 3000 so let's continue. We will now install our fan control knob onto the spray gun. When doing this, make sure that the notch on the fan control knob is facing in the upright position. Put the bushing on the Allen wrench, followed by the spring and the needle packing. Then install through the back of the spray gun using the Allen wrench. Slide the trigger in place and align the holes on the spray gun. Follow by installing the pins through the spray gun. Be sure to slide in the washer onto the top pin. Then reinstall the C-clips. The next step will require us to lubricate a few of the parts. Now spray gun lubricant will be applied to the fluid needle, spring, and fluid control knob. The fluid nozzle is installed and tightened with the spanner wrench. The fluid needle is installed through the back of the gun, along with the spring, and fluid control knob. Finally install the air cap by turning it clockwise onto the spray gun. Before tightening completely, make sure the SATA logo is always in a position that will be readable when the spray gun is in the upright position. After the rebuild process trigger the gun to make sure it is operating properly. Before utilizing it in the refinish process make some spray test patterns.
